Over

User Experience Design
Summer 2016, Cape Town, South Africa
Overview

Over is an iOS app startup with a mission to make design as accessible and easy as possible for people of every age and skill type, while offering the power and tools to be able to create professional level media.

Using the idea of progressive disclosure, more advanced tools can be discovered as the user experiments more with the app. I worked in a team of three interns this past summer to research and design the flows and interface wireframes for the new animation feature the company wanted to introduce. Our work was then passed on to the designer for hi-fidelity mockups and now is being engineering for an upcoming release.

The Task

To add animation functionality to the three main areas of the app: Personal and Stock Libraries, Text, and Artist Artwork. Within each category, animation existed in a very different context. For photo libraries, it meant the ability to import your own GIFs or search for one using a Giphy integration. For text it meant the ability to animate the display patterns of text and its fill, as well as being able to draw a custom text path to animate over. And for Artist Artwork, it meant the ability to download individual or packaged content offered in the app to then edit and place on your project canvas. Since our job was to add a feature to an already existent app, we had to work under the constraints of a preexisting interface language and company values. The Founder and CEO, Aaron Marshall, stressed to us that Over should be like a guitar rather than a violin, easily played by many with the ability to be mastered. The goal was to add this technically advanced feature while making the experience seem intuitive and familiar.

The Process

In our UX team, we performed competitive analysis on current photo app offerings that featured animation and coloring features and presented our synthesis to the full time team including CEO, COO, and Head of Product. Once we agreed upon pursuing the animation feature, we started developing flows that introduced this entirely new interaction to app while keeping familiar gestures and patterns with users in mind. We had daily meetings with the Head of Product, who we reported to directly, but the majority of the delivered work was created completely on our own. Once we settled on user flows, we developed clickable wireframe prototypes using Sketch and Invision that we again presented to the entire team. After a second set of formal feedback, we fine-tuned and continued to test our wireframes before delivering them to the Product & Design team.

Deliverables

We produced and presented competitive analysis of other popular photo editing apps that offered GIF creating or GIF adding features. We developed sketches and wireframes of our own individual flows for the three areas, Libraries, Text, and Artwork, before deciding and combining our work to create the final flows. And we iterated upon our initial flows until we arrived at results we agreed both fit in with the existing patterns and design of the app while effortlessly introduced new and seasoned users to this new experience.
